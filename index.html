Week 2 : CROWDSOURCING

The project called Dead Drops, is an anonymous, offline, peer-to-peer file sharing network in public spaces. Basically, there are USB flash drives stuck into walls, buildings, and curbs for anyone to access. Users are encouraged to drop their files into the flash drives (dead drops) and can connect their laptops to share or receive data, which is stored on a read.me file. So essently, this project is a way for people in the area of the dead drop to anonymously share their data with others and receive data from others. The article written by McCarthy is a project or experiment where the participant is taking information from an outside source, and they must do what they are told to do guide conversations with strangers. Participants are on a date, their actions and decisisions are essentially guided by narrators. To answer this weeks question on how the project; Dead Drops relates to the main topic covered in the experiment done by McCarthy. Dead Drops relates to the article written by McCarthy in ways that informtation is shared via peer to peer communication. Information sent from one user is received to another user through a peer to peer network. For example, in the dating simulator, the participant is receiving directions from an individual on how they should react or act during the date. This individual is receiving information and outputing what they received. In the same regard, Dead Drops, people from all over the world can share information to those who choose to receive the information and vice versa. In Dead Drops, since the flash drives are in walls, buildings and curbs, it means that anyone can access these drives, thus meaning information is to be received and transfered. Consequently, in correlation to the dating experiment, information is received then transfered to the other individual either through conversations or actions, and anyone in the world can participate.
